Russian Solyanka Soup

Solyanka (also spelled soljanka) is a Russian soup that dates back to the 15th century.
The recipe has been adapted and changed over the years to accommodate what meats and vegetables are available,
and like many time-tested dishes, there are several renditions.

Ingredients

For the Soup

  • Large bay leaves
  • Black peppercorns
  • Whole allspice berries
  • Water
  • Concentrated beef base
  • Medium head green cabbage, shredded
  • Coarsely chopped celery
  • Vegetable oil
  • Medium onions, coarsely chopped
  • Medium carrots, peeled and coarsely chopped
  • Dried Polish kabanosy sausage
  • Cooked chicken breasts, cubed
  • Ham, cubed
  • Large dill pickles, coarsely chopped
  • Can tomato paste
  • Cans stewed or diced tomatoes
  • Sliced black olives
  • Capers
  • Dry white wine
  • Kosher sal
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Steps to Make It

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Make a spice sachet by adding the bay leaves, peppercorns, and allspice in a square of cheesecloth.
    Tie with a long piece of butcher's twine.
  3. Using the ends of the butcher's twine,
    tie the sachet onto the handle of a large soup pot so that the sachet hangs into the pot.
  4. Add the water and beef base to the soup pot and bring to a boil.
    Add the shredded cabbage and chopped celery and return to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  5. Heat the oil in a large skillet. Add the onions and carrots and sauté until translucent.
    Add the sausage, chicken, ham, pickles, and tomato paste. Bring to a boil, reduce the heat, and simmer for 2 minutes.

    Add the sausage, chicken, ham, pickles, and tomato paste. Bring to a boil, reduce the heat, and simmer for 2 minutes.

  6. Add the stewed tomatoes, sliced olives, and capers, and bring it barely to a boil.
    Add the wine and simmer gently for 20 minutes.
  7. Remove the sachet and discard. Adjust the seasonings with salt and pepper if necessary.
  8. Ladle the soup into hot bowls and garnish with the sour cream and dill. Serve hot.
